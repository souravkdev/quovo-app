## 1. Problem Statement

Every freelancer faces the same nightmare:

- Client sends: *"Hey can you make me a website? Something modern and clean."*
- Freelancer spends 3 hours on back-and-forth emails
- Project starts with vague expectations
- Client says mid-project: *"I thought this was included"*
- Freelancer either eats the cost or loses the client

**Scope creep is the #1 reason freelancers lose money.** It happens because project briefs are either missing entirely or are informal and unenforceable. Writing a proper brief manually takes 30–60 minutes per project.

---

## 2. Solution

CreatorBrief lets a freelancer:

1. **Paste** a raw client message (WhatsApp, email, DM — anything)
2. **Get** a full professional brief in under 10 seconds
3. **Share** a unique link with the client to review
4. **Get approval** with a single click — timestamped and recorded

The entire flow takes under 2 minutes. The result looks like it was written by a $200/hr project manager.

## 5. Why Not Just Use ChatGPT?

This is the most important question. Here is the honest answer:

| Capability | ChatGPT | CreatorBrief |
|---|---|---|
| Generate a brief | Yes | Yes |
| Shareable client link | No | Yes |
| Client approval button | No | Yes |
| Timestamped approval record | No | Yes |
| Your brief history dashboard | No | Yes |
| Duplicate and reuse past briefs | No | Yes |
| PDF with your logo/branding | No | Yes |
| Remembers your rate & niche | No | Yes |
| Designed for this specific task | No | Yes |

**The key insight:** ChatGPT gives you text in a chat window. CreatorBrief gives you a professional workflow with a client-facing output, approval tracking, and a history of every project. People pay for the workflow, not the AI.

Nobody pays for AI. They pay for outcomes.

## 6. Product Features

### 6.1 Free Tier

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Brief generation | 5 briefs per calendar month |
| Shareable links | Public URL for each brief |
| Basic templates | 3 pre-built templates (web dev, design, content) |
| Brief history | Last 5 briefs visible |
| CreatorBrief branding | "Made with CreatorBrief" footer on all links |

### 6.2 Pro Tier — $12/month

Everything in Free, plus:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Unlimited briefs | No monthly cap |
| PDF export | Download any brief as a formatted PDF |
| Custom branding | Add your logo to brief links and PDFs |
| Client e-approval | Approval button with timestamp record |
| Unlimited history | All briefs saved forever |
| Custom templates | Save your own brief structures |
| Email notifications | Get notified when client approves |

### 6.3 Agency Tier — $29/month

Everything in Pro, plus:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| 5 team seats | Multiple team members under one account |
| White-label links | briefs.youragency.com (custom subdomain) |
| Analytics dashboard | Approval rates, time-to-approval, top clients |
| Priority support | 24hr response time |
| Bulk brief creation | Generate briefs for multiple clients at once |

### 6.4 Feature: AI Brief Generator

The core engine. Input:
- Client's raw message (required)
- Freelancer type (optional: web developer, designer, etc.)
- Your hourly rate (optional — used to calculate price estimate)
- Extra context (optional: deadline, budget the client mentioned)

Output:
- Project title
- 2–3 sentence summary
- Deliverables list
- Timeline estimate
- Number of revision rounds
- Price range (min–max)
- Assumptions (what the brief assumes to be true)
- Out of scope items (explicit list of what is NOT included)
- Next steps for the client

### 6.5 Feature: Shareable Brief Link

- Every brief gets a unique, permanent public URL
- Format: `creatorbrief.com/brief/abc123xyz`
- No login required for the client to view
- Clean, professional layout — looks like a real proposal
- Mobile responsive

### 6.6 Feature: Client Approval

- "I approve this brief" button visible on the public link
- On click: records timestamp, IP address, and approval status
- Freelancer dashboard shows green "Approved" badge
- Approval cannot be undone (protects the freelancer)

### 6.7 Feature: PDF Export (Pro)

- One-click PDF download from any brief
- Includes freelancer logo (if uploaded)
- Professional layout with your name/business name
- Useful for clients who want a document attachment over email

### 6.8 Feature: Brief History Dashboard

- List of all past briefs with date, client message preview, and approval status
- Filter by approved / pending / expired
- Duplicate any past brief to reuse for a similar project
- Search across all past briefs

## 7. User Journey

### New User — First Brief

```
1. Lands on creatorbrief.com
2. Sees demo: messy WhatsApp message → polished brief in 10 seconds
3. Signs up (Google OAuth — one click)
4. Pastes first client message
5. Sees generated brief immediately
6. Copies shareable link
7. Sends link to client
8. Client approves → freelancer gets notified
9. Freelancer is impressed → upgrades to Pro
```

### Returning Pro User — Daily Usage

```
1. Opens dashboard
2. Sees brief history — 3 pending approvals, 2 approved
3. Clicks "New Brief"
4. Pastes new client message
5. Customizes one or two fields
6. Sends link
7. Downloads PDF for their own records
```

### Client Experience (No Login Required)

```
1. Receives link from freelancer
2. Opens link on phone or desktop
3. Reads the brief — clear, professional, well structured
4. Clicks "I approve this brief"
5. Sees confirmation: "Your approval has been recorded."
6. Done — no signup, no friction
```

## 10. Growth Strategy

### Phase 1 — Launch (Week 4–6)

- Post on **ProductHunt** — freelancer tools get strong upvotes
- Share demo video on **Twitter/X** (paste messy message → polished brief in 10 seconds — this is extremely shareable)
- Post in **r/freelance**, **r/webdev**, **r/graphic_design**
- Submit to **IndieHackers** and **BetaList**

### Phase 2 — Organic (Month 2–4)

- SEO content: "how to write a freelance project brief", "scope creep prevention"
- Every public brief link is a marketing touchpoint — clients see the product
- Ask happy users to tweet about it (offer 1 free month)

### Phase 3 — Community (Month 4+)

- Partner with freelance communities (Contra, Toptal forums, Freelancer.com)
- Create a free "Freelance Brief Templates" pack — free lead magnet
- YouTube tutorial: "How I stopped scope creep as a freelancer"

### Viral Loop

```
Freelancer creates brief → shares link with client
→ client sees "Made with CreatorBrief"
→ client is also a freelancer → signs up
→ repeat
```

This built-in viral loop is intentional. The free tier is not just a freemium plan — it's a distribution channel.

## 11. Competitive Landscape

| Product | What it does | Why CreatorBrief wins |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|
| ChatGPT / Claude | General AI chat | No workflow, no shareable output, no approval |
| Bonsai | Full freelance contracts | Too complex, expensive ($24+/mo), overkill |
| HoneyBook | CRM + proposals | Overwhelming for solo freelancers, $19+/mo |
| Google Docs | Manual brief writing | Takes 45 min, no AI, no client approval flow |
| Notion | Manual templates | No AI generation, no shareable approval links |

**CreatorBrief's position:** Fast, focused, AI-powered — for the freelancer who needs a brief in 2 minutes, not a full CRM.

## 12. Success Metrics (KPIs)

### Acquisition

- Signups per week
- Source of signups (ProductHunt, Twitter, SEO, organic)

### Activation

- % of new users who generate at least 1 brief within 24 hours (target: 60%)
- % of users who share a brief link within first session (target: 40%)

### Retention

- Day 7 retention (target: 35%)
- Day 30 retention (target: 20%)
- Monthly active brief creators

### Revenue

- MRR and MRR growth rate
- Free-to-paid conversion rate (target: 5–8%)
- Churn rate (target: <5% monthly for Pro)
- Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)

### Product

- Average time from signup to first brief generated (target: <3 minutes)
- Brief approval rate (% of shared briefs that get approved)
- NPS score (target: 40+)
